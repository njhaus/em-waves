Welcome to How Electromagnetic Waves Work.com!

6 South students have been researching the electromagnetic spectrum and the types of waves that make up the spectrum. As a final project, students chose either to research a type of technology that uses electromagnetic waves or to design an invention that would use electromagnetic waves. Click the tabs above to explore the student-made websites that share our research and inventions. Enjoy!

Electromagnetic waves are a type of energy. They are made of vibrating electric and magnetic fields. Electromagnetic Waves travel at the speed of light through any medium, or through the empty vaccuum of space!

The type of electromagnetic wave depends on the frequency of the wave. The frequency of a wave is measured by how many waves it makes in one second. The types of EM wave from lowest frequency to highest are: radio waves, micro waves, infrared light, visible light, ultraviolet light, x-rays, and gamma rays.

Our modern lives depend on eltromagnetic waves. Technology such as cell phones, wireless internet, and even radios use electromagneitc waves. To learn about more technologies that use electromagnetic waves, keep exploring the rest of this site!
